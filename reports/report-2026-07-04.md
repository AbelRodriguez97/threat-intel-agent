### 🔴 CRITICAL — `CVE-2026-48558`

**SimpleHelp Authentication Bypass Vulnerability** — SimpleHelp  / SimpleHelp

📅 Added to KEV: 2026-06-29 · ⏳ CISA due date: 2026-07-02 · 💀 Ransomware: Unknown

#### Executive summary

A critical authentication bypass vulnerability exists in SimpleHelp's OIDC authentication flow. This flaw allows a remote, unauthenticated attacker to forge identity tokens, gaining full authenticated technician sessions and potentially bypassing multi-factor authentication. Organizations using SimpleHelp with OIDC configured are at severe risk of unauthorized access.

#### Affected technologies

SimpleHelp, OpenID Connect (OIDC)

#### Recommended actions

  1. Immediately apply all available vendor-provided mitigations and patches for SimpleHelp.
  2. Prioritize the update or mitigation of internet-exposed SimpleHelp instances, adhering to CISA's BOD 26-04 guidance.
  3. If mitigations are unavailable, discontinue the use of SimpleHelp in configurations utilizing OIDC authentication.
  4. Implement CISA’s “Forensics Triage Requirements” for any affected systems.
  5. Review and verify OIDC configuration in SimpleHelp to ensure token signature verification is enforced.

#### Rationale

_This vulnerability is classified as critical due to the ease of exploitation by a remote, unauthenticated attacker to achieve full authentication bypass and gain a technician session. The ability to forge identity tokens without signature verification, combined with the potential to bypass multi-factor authentication, represents a severe security risk. Its inclusion in the CISA KEV catalog signifies known active exploitation in the wild._
